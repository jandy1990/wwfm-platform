## Notable Trends & Insights

### 1. Rising Anxiety & Mental Health Concerns
- 43% feel more anxious in 2024 vs 2023
- ADHD most searched mental health topic
- 30% of Americans lonely weekly
- Gen Z (41%) and Millennials (36%) report higher mental health struggles

### 2. Workplace Crisis
- 57% experiencing moderate-to-high burnout
- 90% feel workplace stress
- 44% considering quitting within 6 months
- Job searching difficulty (some filling 1,000+ applications)

### 3. Financial Stress Universal
- 84% feel financial stress
- 41% cite inflation as top problem (3rd year in a row)
- 60% cite cost as barrier to healthy eating
- Housing and food costs primary stressors

### 4. Health Information Seeking
- Contagious illness questions dominate (bronchitis, pneumonia, strep, flu)
- Chronic condition management (cholesterol, blood pressure, A1C)
- Symptom understanding (lightheadedness most searched)
- Mental health integration with physical health

### 5. Relationship Complexity
- Therapy-speak dominates (gaslighting, narcissism, codependence)
- Alternative relationship structures explored (open relationships, ethical non-monogamy)
- Relationship viability questions common ("is my relationship over?")

### 6. Weight & Appearance Concerns Persistent
- "Lose weight fast" remains top fitness search
- Hair loss major concern (25,945 monthly searches)
- Skin problems (acne, pores, dark circles)
- Quick fixes sought despite expert warnings

### 7. Skill Development Focus
- AI/ML fastest growing (40% job growth expected)
- Communication remains #1 in-demand skill
- Personal enrichment motivation (84% for language learning)
- Practical skills (cooking, baking) popular

### 8. Youth-Specific Challenges
- Higher anxiety rates (26.6% ages 18-29 vs 11.2% ages 65+)
- Vaping addiction concerns (48% considering quitting)
- Social media impact
- Career uncertainty
